The Story
人生章節
Taiwan era
2013 — 2017 · Taipei, Taiwan

Half a million people
showed up. She made sure
they had something to find.

五十萬人湧入，
她確保每個人
都能找到他們要的東西。

Before UX, there was mass communication at a very literal scale. Dianne directed marketing campaigns for shopping centers like Shin Kong Mitsukoshi, coordinating everything from in-store signage to 500,000-copy print runs. She learned early on that good design isn't about how things look — it's about whether people notice, understand, and act.

在 UX 之前，是真正大規模的大眾傳播。Dianne 在新光三越等百貨公司負責行銷活動，從店內視覺到印刷量超過五十萬份的宣傳品，一手統籌。她很早就明白：好的設計不是關於視覺，而是人們是否真的看到、理解、並採取行動。

Dance studio era
2017 — 2020 · Seattle

She co-founded a dance
studio. It grew 150%.
People kept coming back.

她共同創辦了一間舞蹈教室。
學員成長了 150%，
大家一直回來。

Five years running Lindy Island Swing Dance Studio taught Dianne something no design school could: how to read a room. Literally. You notice when someone's confused, when they're bored, when something suddenly clicks. That instinct — for friction, for timing, for when to step back — is exactly what she brings to every product she works on.

五年經營 Lindy Island 搖擺舞教室，教會了 Dianne 任何設計學院都給不了的東西：如何讀懂一個空間。當有人感到困惑、無聊，或突然「懂了」的瞬間——你能感覺到。這種對摩擦、節奏、以及何時退一步的本能，她帶進了每一個產品設計裡。

Design era
BA · Film & Graphic Design

She learned to tell stories
with pictures and timing.
Then she never stopped.

她學會了用畫面和節奏說故事，
然後從未停下來過。

A BA in Film and Graphic Design gave Dianne two things: a deep respect for composition and rhythm, and the confidence to tell stories without words. Every interface she designs still carries that sensibility — where does the eye go first? What comes next? What's the last thing you feel before you leave?

電影與視覺設計學位給了 Dianne 兩件事：對構圖與節奏的深刻理解，以及不靠文字說故事的能力。她設計的每個介面都帶著這種感覺——眼睛先去哪裡？接下來是什麼？離開前的最後感受是什麼？

Seattle era
Master's UX · Seattle, WA · Now

A Master's in UX. A city
she fell for. And now,
building with AI.

UX 碩士學位。一座讓她著迷的城市。
現在，她用 AI 一起建造。

The Master's sharpened how Dianne connects stories to real people's needs. Seattle gave her the startup ecosystem to apply it. Now she's exploring the edge of what's next: designing in Figma and building with Claude — because the gap between "I designed it" and "it exists" shouldn't require a whole engineering team.

碩士學位讓 Dianne 更精準地將故事與真實需求連結。西雅圖給了她可以實踐的新創生態系。現在她在探索下一步的邊界：在 Figma 設計，用 Claude 建造——因為從「我設計了它」到「它存在了」，不應該還需要一整個工程團隊。

How Dianne Works
工作方式
01
Start with the friction
從摩擦點開始

Before wireframes, before pixels — Dianne asks where things break down. What's the moment users hesitate, backtrack, or give up? That's where design begins.

在線框圖和像素之前，Dianne 先問：哪裡會出問題？使用者猶豫、走回頭路、或放棄的那個瞬間——那才是設計的起點。

02
Design × AI, together
設計 × AI，一起

Dianne designs in Figma and builds with Claude. AI isn't a shortcut — it's a collaborator that lets her close the gap between concept and reality, faster and more completely.

Dianne 在 Figma 設計，用 Claude 建造。AI 不是捷徑——它是協作者，讓她更快、更完整地縮短概念與現實之間的距離。

Figma → Claude → Live
03
The camera changes everything
相機改變了一切

Photography taught Dianne to make decisions in a fraction of a second — light, framing, timing. That same instinct shapes how she chooses between ten nearly-identical design options.

攝影教會 Dianne 在幾分之一秒內做出決定——光線、構圖、時機。同樣的直覺，讓她在十個幾乎一模一樣的設計選項中做出判斷。
